About me




My name is Ganesh Biswa. I was born in march 9, 1999. I have 2 older sisters along with 1 little brother. My parents were born and raised in Bhutan. But my birth was in Nepal in a small refugee camp called Khudhuna Bari. There were more than 20,000 people. Life in a camp was hard because of situations. It was hard for my parent to provide food and clothes for us. Sometimes we would sleep without eating. My parents were only able to buy us new cloth once a year. Even though life was hard, everyone in the camp were happy with what we had.

In 2008, we start hearing about IOM. It is an international organization for migration to help all refugees to give them new life in different countires around the world. My parents did not wanted to leave Nepal. But in 2012, my parents finally sign paperworks to come to United States. The process took over a year. We had to go through lot of history and medical check up. Therefore, we landed in USA on January 22, 2013.

I love playing soccer and listening to music. I have broken my hand couple of times playing soccer back in Nepal. We played soccer with plastic balls which is really fun to play while raining. I usually listen to bollyhood musics because they are my favorite. I had lots of friends back in Nepal. One of the thing that really disappointed me in the camp was the poor medical support. We did not had good hospital or pharmacy. Our hospitals were made out bambooes and it could not fit much people. As a child, all the situation and issues in the camp allowed me to focus lot toward my education.

Education

My school life started in camp. I started my first grade when I was 7 years old. When I came to the United States I was already in 8 grade. Following are the list of schools I have attended or attending: Druck Model School (2006 - 2011), Sunrise Acadamy School (2011 - 2013), Lewis and Clark Middle School (2013), Central High School (2013 - 2017), UNO (2017 - Present)

Scool in the United States for me brough whole in world to me. It is better than school I went back in the camp. There are millions of opportunities to be successful in life.
